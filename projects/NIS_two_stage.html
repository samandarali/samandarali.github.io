Conceptual Model: National Innovation System (Two-Stage Network)

Innovation Inputs

  • Human Capital: % educated above Bachelor’s; adult literacy
  • Infrastructure: ICT Access Index; QS top-3 universities average score
  • R&D: Gross R&D expenditure (% of GDP)

Commercialization Inputs

  • Intermediate inputs: articles + patents
  • Domestic credit to private sector: % of GDP
  • High-/medium-skill labor: % employed in high/medium-tech industries
Stage 1
Innovation Creation (Invention Stage)
Intermediate Outputs
Scientific & technical articles
Patent applications
Stage 2
Innovation Commercialization (Market / Economic Stage)

Outputs of Stage 1 become inputs to Stage 2 (connected/series network).

Final System Outputs

Economic Outcomes
  • Economic Complexity Index (ECI)
  • Industrial Value Added (manufacturing value added per capita)

Environmental Factors (External Drivers)

  • Good Governance Index: rule of law, regulatory quality, control of corruption, government effectiveness, voice & accountability, political stability
  • Customs tariffs: weighted mean tariff rate
  • University–industry collaboration: research cooperation metric
  • Total population: market size proxy
  • Utility model law: dummy variable for “small patents” protection

These factors influence efficiency (tested via Tobit regression) but are not part of the two-stage production flow.
